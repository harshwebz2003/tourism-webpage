Seasonal Guide

Best Time to Visit

Sri Lanka has two distinct monsoon seasons. The west & south coasts are best from December–April, while the east is best from May–September.

☀️
Dec – Apr

Peak Season

  • South & West Coast ideal
  • Best whale watching
  • Cultural triangle perfect
  • Clear skies & dry days
Best: Mirissa, Galle, Sigiriya
🌿
May – Sep

East Coast Season

  • Trincomalee & Arugam Bay open
  • World-class surfing
  • Hill country lush & green
  • Fewer crowds, lower rates
Best: Arugam Bay, Trinco, Ella
🌦️
Oct – Nov

Shoulder Season

  • Inter-monsoon period
  • Excellent wildlife sightings
  • Yala & Udawalawe best
  • Budget-friendly deals
Best: Yala, Minneriya, Kandy

Year-round paradise — Sri Lanka's tropical climate means there's always something beautiful to discover.

FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your Sri Lanka adventure.

Most nationalities require an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) which can be obtained online in minutes before arrival. The standard tourist ETA allows a 30-day stay (extendable to 90 days) and costs approximately $35 USD.

December to April is ideal for the south and west coasts (Mirissa, Galle, Colombo). May to September is perfect for the east coast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay). The hill country (Ella, Kandy) is beautiful year-round, with the most dramatic greenery during the monsoons.

Sri Lanka is considered very safe for solo travellers, including women travelling alone. The locals are exceptionally warm and hospitable. Standard travel precautions apply, and our team provides 24/7 emergency support for all bookings.

We recommend a minimum of 10–14 days to experience the main highlights. A week covers the cultural triangle and one coast. Three weeks gives you time to explore the island in depth — from the northern wildlife reserves to the deep south beaches.

The Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR) is the official currency. Major credit cards are accepted at hotels and tourist spots. ATMs are widely available in cities and tourist areas. We recommend carrying some cash for local markets and tuk-tuk rides.
